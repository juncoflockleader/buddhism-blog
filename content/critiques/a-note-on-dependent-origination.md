# A Note on Dependent Origination

The twelve-nidāna formula — from *avijjā* (ignorance) through *jarāmaraṇa* (ageing and death) — is commonly read as a description of rebirth across multiple lives. On this cosmological reading, the first two links (ignorance and formations) belong to a past life; the next eight to the present; and the final two (birth, ageing-and-death) to a future life.

This "three-life" interpretation is standard in the Theravāda commentarial tradition and is powerfully present in the Visuddhimagga. But is it the most natural reading of the suttas themselves?

## The Experiential Reading

Several passages in the Nidāna Saṃyutta (SN 12) suggest a present-moment, experiential reading. In SN 12.23 (*Upanisā Sutta*), dependent origination is framed not as a cosmological process but as the arising of dukkha *in experience* — conditioned by craving, which is conditioned by feeling, and so on back to contact and ignorance.

Importantly, the suttas frequently present the formula in reverse (*paṭiloma*): understanding how cessation works *now* by tracing the chain backward is the practice. This strongly implies that the chain describes something observable in one's own experience, not a narrative about previous lives.

## The Commentarial Inflation

The three-life interpretation requires us to read "birth" (*jāti*) as biological birth and "consciousness" (*viññāṇa*) as a re-linking consciousness at conception. This is a significant interpretive move that goes well beyond what the terms mean in their non-Abhidhammic usages in the Nikāyas.

*Jāti* in the suttas also regularly means the arising of a sense of self around a given object — "there is born in him the conceit 'I am'." The psychological reading is at least as well attested as the biological one.

## Conclusion

Neither reading is obviously wrong; both have textual support. But when teaching the practice, the experiential reading has a significant advantage: it makes dependent origination *verifiable*. You can watch how ignorance conditions a proliferating response to sense contact right now, in this sitting. The three-life reading, whatever its metaphysical merits, removes the doctrine from direct investigation.

This is not to say the commentarial tradition is mistaken — only that it has privileged one reading and presented it as *the* reading.
